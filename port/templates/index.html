About

My Aim is to turn Your Vision to Visual Reality

I'm Iyanu Popoola, a UI/UX Product Designer focused on designing clear, user-centred, and intuitive mobile applications and web experiences. I work end-to-end. from discovery and user research to wireframes, high-fidelity UI, and interactive prototyping with a strong emphasis on usability, clarity, and accessibility.

I’m experienced in designing quickly and independently in Figma, collaborating within Agile teams, and presenting design decisions to stakeholders. My work balances user needs with real technical constraints, and I enjoy turning complex problems into simple, intuitive interfaces. I’ve also worked on early-stage and fast-moving products, including AI-driven flows such as chatbots and automation.

My approach is structured, thoughtful, and detail-driven, Always focused on building products that feel easy to use and purposeful.

Common Questions

Working with me.

I believe in transparency and clear communication. Here are some things clients and employers usually ask about my process.

Still have questions?
What is your design process? +

I begin with research to understand users and goals. Then, I define the problem, create wireframes and prototypes, test with users, and iterate. I make sure my designs align with business needs and user expectations. I collaborate with developers during handoff and ensure the final product reflects the intended experience.

How do you handle feedback and communication with teams and stakeholders? +

I set regular check-ins to share design progress and gather input. I present ideas clearly often with visuals to help others understand the reasoning. When feedback comes in, I ask questions to clarify and apply it thoughtfully. My goal is to keep everyone aligned and make sure the design meets user and business needs.

Tell us about how you balance user needs with business goals. +

I always research both sides first. Then, I find design solutions that meet user needs while supporting business objectives like conversions or engagement. For example, improving a checkout flow can help users and also reduce drop-off. I use data to back up decisions and test designs to ensure they're working for both users and the business.

How do you stay updated with design trends and evolving user behaviors? +

I follow design blogs, newsletters, Youtube channels and podcasts. I explore updates in Figma, attend webinars, and join design communities. I also observe user behavior trends in reports or usability studies. Staying updated helps me create designs that feel fresh and meet user expectations as they change.

How do you measure the success of your designs? +

I measure success through user feedback, usability tests, and performance metrics—like task completion, engagement, or conversion rates. I also check if business goals are being met. For example, if a new onboarding flow reduces drop-offs, I know it worked. I track this data and use it to make design decisions in future iterations.

Have you ever worked in Agile or cross-functional teams? How did you adapt your process? +

Yes, l've worked in Agile squads with developers, PMs, and QA. I break designs into small, testable parts to match sprints. I attend daily standups and sprint reviews to stay synced. I design ahead of the sprint when needed and adapt based on feedback. Agile keeps things fast-paced, so | learned to stay flexible and focused.
